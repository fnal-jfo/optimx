This element scatters particles both transversely and longitudinally. Transversely, small angle Coulomb scattering is approximated by a Gaussian distribution while large scattering is modeled by a $1/\theta^3$ distribution consistent with the anglular dependence of the single particle Rutherford scattering cross-section. The longitudinal distribution is modeled by a Vavilov distribution. Every particle gets an independent random (Gaussian distributed) kick. The first parameter specifies the scattering rms angular spread. The second parameter is the relative thickness gradient (for ionization cooling wedged absorber). The third parameter is the wedge tilt (roll).
